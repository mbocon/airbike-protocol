THE THREE SYSTEMS YOU'RE TRAINING

1. Aerobic Base (Zone 2) — "The Engine"
Builds mitochondrial density, capillary networks, cardiac stroke volume, and fat oxidation. This is 75–80% of your total training volume. Without this foundation, HIIT gains plateau within weeks. Research (Seiler, 2010) shows elite endurance athletes spend 80% of their time here — and you should too.
2. VO₂max (Norwegian 4×4) — "The Ceiling"
The 4×4 protocol directly raises your maximum oxygen uptake by forcing the heart to pump at near-maximal stroke volume for sustained periods. Helgerud et al. (2007) showed 11% VO₂max improvement in 8 weeks. The Wen et al. (2018) meta-analysis confirmed that intervals longer than 2 minutes with total high-intensity volume exceeding 15 minutes per session are optimal for VO₂max development.
3. Lactate Threshold — "The Sustainable Speed"
Training at 80–88% HRmax in sustained blocks (5–8 min) improves your body's ability to clear lactate and sustain higher intensities before 'hitting the wall.' This determines how long you can work at a high percentage of your VO₂max. Over-under intervals (Phase 4) further train lactate buffering by oscillating above and below threshold.
